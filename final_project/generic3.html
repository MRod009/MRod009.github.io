Barcelona

Boasting a reputation as one of the most attractive cities in Europe, Barcelona celebrates its role as the capital of Catalonia. The city’s cosmopolitan international vibe makes it a favorite city for many people and is known for its architecture and art. Travelers flock from around the world to see the iconic Sagrada Família church and other modernist landmarks designed by Gaudí. Barcelona is truly a one of a kind vibrant city! For more information on where to stay and what to see please visit Barcelona Travel Guide.

Wondering where to eat in Barcelona? In a city with options ranging from old school Spanish classics, to traditional Catalan fare to foward thinking fusion spots, there’s no short answer. Barcelona is a food lover’s city. Even if you eat six meals a day, as we often eat here in Spain, that’s still not enough time to truly grasp all that’s on offer. From age-old, family-run tavernas to Michelin-starred creative hotspots, the list of where to eat in Barcelona is endless. Whether you’re visiting for a few days or calling Barcelona home long term, be sure to choose wisely! For the top 38 places to eat in the city please visit Barcelona Eater guide.
